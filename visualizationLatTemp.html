Temperature vs Latitude

We all know that places get warmer as you get closer to the equator, right? Well, how does that relationship play out at a very specific time in the year with a lot of cities graphed according to their latitude and temperature?

According to the data and the way it looks, it would appear that the maximum temperature is usually around 100 degrees farenheit, and the center is actually pretty close to the equator before abruptly falling off, even getting as low as -50 degrees farenheit at latitudes of 80, which is northern Canada, Scandinavia, and the Russian tundra.

This is strong supporting evidence for an emerging theory of the earth's shape called "Oblate Spheroid theory", which predicts that "Seasons" would occur - the northern "hemisphere" (half of the sphere!) would be colder as it is angled away from the sun, and the lower hemisphere would be warmer as it was angled toward the sun.
